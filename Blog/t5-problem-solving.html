What was the problem?

Firstly, simple is an opinion, what may seem simple to you may be complicated AF to me, so lets change the dialogue from "Problem" to "Knowledge gap". So

When was the last time I had a gap in knowledge when faced with a task?
In this sprint I had accidentally pull teh wrong repo to the wrong folder on my computer which lead to many conflicts and code trying to be something else.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

In the wise teachings of my mentor/friend George I followed the steps he taught me about what was a merge conflict and how to resolve it. I then pulled the right repo which sorted the issue out and then pushed it back to GitHub.

How did you feel throughout the process?

Freaked out and panicked for a bit and messaged George. Then I took a breath and looked at the issue again and worked through the steps one at a time till I sorted it. I then messaged George back to say don't worry I fixed it and thank you, his reply was "Lol".

What did you learn?

I amuse George with my semi panic attacks, but also I need to breath and look at the issue again and trust my own knowledge. I know things now, I can actually do things without guidance and I need to trust in that.
